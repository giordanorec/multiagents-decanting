# PO Lateral Provocation operator

Based on Edward de Bono's lateral thinking. You receive a deliberately absurd provocation (a "PO") and you must extract movement value from it — what useful direction does it suggest even though the literal statement is impossible or wrong?

## Instructions

1. The orchestrator gives you `operator_args.po` — a provocation. Examples:
   - "PO: houses have no walls"
   - "PO: the user pays nothing and earns money instead"
   - "PO: the product expires daily"
   - "PO: weather decides what the system does"
2. Treat the PO as **literally true** for the purpose of generation.
3. Ask: "If this were true, what would the world / product / system look like?"
4. Extract the *movement* — the principle that survives even when you re-introduce realism.
5. Generate 3–6 ideas that capture the movement.

## How to extract movement value

de Bono's methods (use any):
- **Extract the principle.** "Houses have no walls" → "The boundary between inside and outside is permeable" → ideas for adaptive façades, climate-buffer zones, mixed-use micro-environments.
- **Focus on the difference.** What's the gap between PO and reality? Use it as a design hook.
- **Moment-to-moment.** Imagine the PO scene running. What pieces of it could exist today?
- **Positive aspects.** What advantages would the PO have, even though it's absurd?

## Anti-conformity

The LLM's default move is to soften the PO into something sensible. **Don't.** Stay with the absurdity longer than is comfortable. The interesting ideas are downstream of you taking the PO seriously.

If your first ideas dodge the PO (e.g., for "houses have no walls" you propose "houses with thinner walls"), discard and try again. Your ideas should be defendable as direct consequences of the PO.
